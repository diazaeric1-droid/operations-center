2. **Rig-in pulling unit** — Mobilize Permian WOR pulling unit to location; rig up per well control SOP.
3. **Pull ESP string** — Pull ESP, motor, and production tubing to surface; lay out on pipe racks for post-treatment re-run inspection. *(Note: if motor winding resistance is below spec on pull, initiate separate ESP swap AFE.)*
4. **Wellbore cleanout pass** — Coiled tubing unit (CTU) wash and gauge the wellbore to confirm no fill or obstructions prior to acid placement.
5. **Acid stimulation** — Spot 5,000 gal 15% HCl + non-emulsifier with solid diverter/ball-sealer package via CTU; pump in 1,000 gal incremental stages monitoring injection pressure and ISIP between stages; shut in 2 hrs for reaction.
6. **Flush and displace** — Displace spent acid with KCl brine to prevent secondary corrosion; confirm full fluid returns.
7. **Flowback & disposal** — Flow well through temporary surface equipment; truck produced fluids + spent acid returns to SWD for the duration of clean-up (5 days budgeted).
8. **Re-run production string** — Re-run ESP (existing motor if within spec, or replacement per separate ESP swap AFE); tag depth; make up tubing to surface.
9. **Rig-out** — Release pulling unit; clean location.
10. **Startup & monitoring** — Energize ESP; monitor motor amps, intake pressure, and production rate continuously for first 48 hrs; confirm operating within POR and amps returning to nameplate range.

## Technical Justification

### Primary Diagnosis

ED-001H has been on continuous ESP lift for 990 days. Surveillance trending over the preceding 5 days shows:
- **Motor amps:** +26% over nameplate — consistent with increased hydraulic load caused by scale deposition on rotor/diffuser stages and/or restricted shroud annulus.
- **Intake pressure:** Declining 38 → 31 psi (−18.4% in 5 days) — indicative of progressing near-wellbore scale or formation damage reducing PI, not compressible-reservoir depletion at this rate of change.
- **Operating point:** Pump is operating below published POR floor, elevating radial bearing wear rate and vibration, which are the primary precursors to insulation failure and motor burn.

### Expected Outcome

Acid dissolution of inorganic scale (likely carbonate/sulfate mix given produced water chemistry and ~990-day exposure) is expected to restore intake PI and return the pump to mid-POR operation. The engineering estimate is **+130 BOPD incremental rate**, with a decline rate of **0.75/yr** applied to the uplift, yielding an estimated **59,909 Bbl EUR uplift**.

### Why Now

Continued operation below the POR floor projects motor failure within 60–90 days based on industry experience with similar Wolfcamp ESP installations. Pulling the ESP without prior acid treatment typically produces a re-failure within 3–6 months when scale redeposits on the new motor. Scale treatment must precede any mechanical work.

### Alternatives Considered

| Alternative | Disposition | Reason Rejected |
|---|---|---|
| Squeeze-and-produce scale inhibitor only | Rejected | Does not dissolve existing deposits; PI impairment will persist and pump will remain below POR |
| ESP swap without stimulation | Rejected | High recurrence risk; industry experience on similar Wolfcamp ESP wells shows re-failure within 3–6 months when underlying scale not addressed |
| Rod pump conversion | Rejected | Reservoir depth and fluid volume incompatible; solution GOR unsuitable for rod-pump operation at this location |
| Do nothing / monitor | Rejected | Motor failure is imminent (<90 days projected); emergency workover cost would exceed this AFE 2–3× |
